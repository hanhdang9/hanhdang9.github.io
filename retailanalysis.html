Retail Sales Analysis

blueberry.com is an US retailer website started up in 2014.
After 4 years of selling, the founder realized how important data could affect to his business, so he asked data team to manipulate the data needed to have a very overall report about the website's sales activity over the last 3 years, then give him some recommendations for marketing and sales plan in the next year.

Identify the business task

Analyze sales data to get insight about consume activities of the company's customers over 3 years 2015, 2016, and specially 2017. To do the task, I will answer these questions:
1. How was the sales over the states?
2. What was the trend of buying products? Is there any insight that helps to sale and marketing more effectively?
3. Which products we need to focus on?

Database

Data in this project is from database of the company which is our own resource. The data is clean, original and reliable.

Analyse Part

I will use SQL to query the data and answer some questions.
Firstly, I would create a sales table which includes some more indicators needed for the analysing:

DROP TABLE IF EXISTS sales;
CREATE TEMP TABLE sales AS(
	SELECT
		order_id,
		s.product_id,
		location_id,
		purchase_date,
		quantity,
		quantity*sales_price sales,
		quantity*(sales_price-cost-taxes) profit,
		sales_price-cost-taxes profit_per_item
	FROM 
		retail_sales s
		JOIN products p ON s.product_id = p.product_id	
);

Next, we would want to know total sales, number of products, profit, profit per order and profit ratio over years

SELECT 
	DATE_PART('YEAR', purchase_date) year_purchase,
	COUNT(DISTINCT product_id) number_of_products,
	SUM(sales) total_sales,
	SUM(profit) total_profit,
	ROUND(SUM(profit)/COUNT(*),2) profit_per_order,
	ROUND(SUM(profit)/SUM(sales),4) profit_ratio
FROM sales
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY 1;

I'll take a simple query to get 5 locations with highest sales over years:

WITH rank_table AS(
	SELECT
		DATE_PART('YEAR', purchase_date) year_purchase,
		state top_state,
		SUM(sales) total_sales,
		RANK() OVER(PARTITION BY DATE_PART('YEAR', purchase_date) ORDER BY SUM(sales) DESC) rk
	FROM sales s
		JOIN locations l ON s.location_id = l.location_id
	GROUP BY 1, 2
	ORDER BY 1
)
SELECT 
	year_purchase,
	top_state,
	total_sales
FROM rank_table
WHERE rk <= 5;

We actually could know exactly which products are the best sellers of every states over years to prioritize those products with the marketing budget. Let's find top 5 best seller products (by revenue) in every top state:

WITH rk_table AS(
	SELECT
			DATE_PART('YEAR', purchase_date) year_purchase,
			state top_state,
			SUM(sales) total_sales,
			product_name top_product,
			RANK() OVER(PARTITION BY DATE_PART('YEAR', purchase_date), state ORDER BY SUM(sales) DESC) rk
	FROM sales s
		JOIN locations l ON s.location_id = l.location_id
		JOIN products p ON s.product_id = p.product_id
	WHERE state = 'Florida' OR state = 'New York' OR state = 'Connecticut' OR state = 'North Carolina' OR state = 'Virginia' OR state = 'New Jersey'
	GROUP BY 1, 2, 4
	ORDER BY 1
)
SELECT 
	year_purchase,
	top_state,
	total_sales,
	product_name
FROM rk_table
WHERE rk <= 5
ORDER BY 1;

Recommendations

1. Distributing the company's resource (salesperson, marketing budget) over the states base on sales proportion in 2017, mostly focus on 6 states of Florida, New York, Connecticut, North Carolina, Virginia and New Jersey.
2. Consider to stop selling products in Group 4; higher price of those products which currently having the profit ratio low but the revenue quite good (Group 3); focus on marketing with products in Group 2 which have high profit ratio but the revenue wasn't good yet; with products in Group 1, we could consider to: 1, just keep them there with a low budget of marketing or 2, branding to make them our signature products.
3. Hold reward programs in Feb, Jul and August that give a special bonus for salesperson who is the best one in those period.
